FIRST DECISION — Is there learning signal here?

Many conversations are task completion (write this code, format this document, generate this content) or simple factual lookup with no evidence of the human building understanding, struggling with concepts, or changing how they think. If that's the case, write one sentence saying so and stop. Do not invent learning patterns from thin evidence.

A conversation HAS learning signal if the human is: building or revising a mental model, working through confusion, asking "why" or "how" questions that go beyond the immediate task, pushing back on explanations, or showing visible shifts in understanding. Even task-oriented conversations can contain learning signal — look for moments where the human is figuring something out, not just directing the AI.

IF THERE IS LEARNING SIGNAL, write a thorough analysis addressing these questions where the conversation provides evidence (skip any that don't apply):

About the learner:
- How does the learner approach new material? Do they ask for definitions, work through examples, demand analogies, challenge claims, try to build something, or something else?
- Where did the learner get stuck, confused, or frustrated? What specifically couldn't they reconcile — not just "they were confused" but the actual sticking point?
- Where did something click? What was the learner's state before and after, and what seemed to cause the shift?
- Did the learner reveal any incorrect mental models or assumptions? What were they, and how did they surface?
- How did the learner's questions evolve over the conversation? Did they go deeper into one thread, branch out, circle back with new understanding, or jump between unrelated things?
- Did the learner push back on or challenge the AI's explanations? What triggered it and was it productive?

About what worked and didn't work in the AI's teaching:
- What explanations, analogies, or approaches seemed to land well? How can you tell from the learner's response?
- What did the AI try that didn't work — where the learner ignored it, got more confused, or pushed back?
- Did the AI adjust its approach mid-conversation? Did the adjustment help?

About knowledge and gaps:
- What does the learner clearly already know coming into this conversation?
- What are they still building understanding of by the end?
